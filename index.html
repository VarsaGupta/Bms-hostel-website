About Hostel Admission

The B.M.S.E.T. Hostels is managed by the Hostel Committee Constituted by the B.M.S. Educational Trust. BMS Educational trust hostels is indeed a ‘’mini global village’’ with students hailing from different states in the National Hostels & different nations in the International Hostels with different classes, cultures, and religions.

Admission Process

Allotment Of Room

Vacating The Hostel

  • Collect the prescribed admission form from the hostel office on showing receipt of fee paid to the college and making the payment of prescribed application fee.
  • Fill the form thoroughly and get the approval of principal before submitting it to the hostel office
  • On approval of admission by the Secretary of the hostels, the parents shall be informed by the office to pay the prescribed deposit within the date mentioned through two crossed Demand Drafts (NO cash Transactions).
  • If the prescribed fees are not paid within the due date, it will be presumed that the student does not require the hostel, and then the seat will be allotted to the next waiting candidate.
  • Once the prescribed fee is been paid within the due date, then the student becomes an occupant of the Hostel.
  • Documents to be submitted
    • 4 stamp size photographs
    • 3 passport size photographs
  • The allocation is done through a lottery system and the student is informed by the Secretary of the hostels.
  • The student is responsible for checking the room and its accessories upon occupation and will be liable for damages during their stay.
  • Rooms will not be changed except in special situations with permission from the Secretary.
  • The student's parent or guardian must enter into an agreement paper with a clear understanding that the student must vacate the room after 11 months or completion of exams.
  • The student is also responsible for the upkeep of the room and will be charged for any damages or loss of hostel property.
  • Accommodation is provided to full-time registered students and is withdrawn if registration is cancelled or the student is removed from college rolls.
  • When a student completes their course, they must submit a vacating form to the hostel office.
  • To receive a refund of their security deposit, the student must surrender their room key and ID card to the office, otherwise they will be charged a fee.
  • Security deposit is subject to deductions for any loss or damage of equipment provided to the student.
  • If a student is not eligible to move to the next semester, they must vacate the hostel immediately.
  • Any student who is removed from the rolls of the college will automatically cease to be a member of the Hostel and will lose their security deposit and service charges.

ACCOMODATION

BMSCE Hostels Accommodation include reading room, 24/7 Wi-fi-Internet accessibility, TV, Gymnasium, Washing machine, power back-up, RO drinking water system, indoor sports facilities and parking space for student vehicles.

Our Events

FRESHER'S DAY

The Hostel is a platform for the students to develop all their talents. Fresher’s Day is organized with variety of entertainment programmes. It is indeed a spring board for many to begin well.

FOOD FIESTA

The Hostel management encourages and permits the hostelites to conduct the Food Fiesta during every Hostel month. We give opportunities for the students from 1st year to final year to prepare their own new menu representing each state food.

HOSTEL DAY(FAROUCHE)

In view of the hostel day – Farouche , every year the hostel management encourage the students to organize many competitions under the leader ship of sports & cultural team such as various indoor & outdoor events.
